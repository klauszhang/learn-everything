## Sampling strategies

The probability distribution gives every vocabulary token a score. **Temperature**
reshapes that distribution before sampling — drag the slider to see how:

<SamplingDials client:load />

**Top-k** restricts sampling to only the `k` highest-probability tokens; everything
else is zeroed out and the rest renormalized. **Top-p (nucleus)** instead keeps the
smallest set of tokens whose cumulative probability reaches `p` — adapting
dynamically to the model's confidence. In practice, temperature + top-p are
combined: temperature adjusts the shape, top-p clips the tail.

## Prefill vs. decode

Generation has two phases that behave very differently inside the model. Hit
"Run" to watch the prompt processed in parallel, then tokens generated one at
a time:

<PrefillDecodeSplit client:load />

The asymmetry matters for latency. A 200-token response to a 1,000-token prompt
means one fast prefill pass followed by 200 sequential decode steps — each one
re-reading the cached K and V vectors for all prior tokens.

**Generation is the transformer's output loop.** The model produces one token at a time: the full sequence runs through every layer, the final hidden state at the last position is projected into a probability distribution over the vocabulary, and one token is sampled. That token is appended to the sequence and the process repeats. Temperature, top-p, and top-k shape the sampling — they control the tradeoff between coherence and diversity without changing the model's learned distribution.

</CacheCallout>
