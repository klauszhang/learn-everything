## The problem

Autoregressive decoding generates one token at a time. Without optimization,
each new token forces the model to recompute K and V for the entire prefix
through every layer — O(n²) total work. The KV cache eliminates that by
storing K and V after their first computation.

The KV cache is the transformer's key inference optimization. K and V vectors
for prompt tokens don't change between decode steps — they depend only on
position and weights. Storing them after prefill eliminates redundant
computation and turns quadratic decode cost into linear.
