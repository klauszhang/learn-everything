## From one block to a full model

Stack 32–96 copies of the block from Ch 4 and you have a full transformer.
Same structure every time, different learned weights. The same token vector
passes through every block — by the final layer, a simple word embedding has
become a rich, context-dependent representation tuned for next-token prediction.

Hover a block to see what happens at that depth — and how the word "bank"
evolves differently depending on whether it's next to "river" or "savings":

<LayerStack client:load />

After the final block, the last token's vector is projected into a score for
every token in the vocabulary — these scores are called **logits**. Softmax
turns them into probabilities, and one token is sampled. Ch 6 walks through
this pipeline.

**Depth is how the transformer builds abstraction.** Each layer refines the representation: early layers capture syntax and local patterns, later layers capture semantics and long-range dependencies. The residual connection is what makes this depth practical — it gives gradients a clean path back through dozens or hundreds of layers during training, and lets each block focus on learning a small incremental transformation rather than reconstructing the full representation from scratch.
